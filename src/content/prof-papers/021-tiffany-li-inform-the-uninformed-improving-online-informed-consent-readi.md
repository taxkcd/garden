- **Year:** 2023

## Paper overview

This paper studies how an AI-powered chatbot named Rumi can improve the process of online informed consent by guiding participants through consent forms interactively. The chatbot helps participants better understand and recall consent information, promotes a more equal power relationship between participants and researchers, and improves the quality of participants' responses in a subsequent survey study.

### Why it matters

**Research problem:** Participants often do not thoroughly read or understand online informed consent forms, leading to uninformed decisions and potential risks for both participants and researchers. Traditional online consent processes lack researcher presence and guidance, which reduces comprehension and engagement.

**Why it matters:** Uninformed consent can expose participants to unknown risks, harm their autonomy, and reduce study validity and data quality. With the rise of online studies, especially during the COVID-19 pandemic, improving online informed consent is critical for ethical and effective research.

**Key contributions:**

- Systematic comparison of AI-powered chatbot-driven informed consent with traditional online consent forms.
- Empirical evidence that chatbot improves consent form recall, comprehension, and reduces power asymmetry between participant and researcher.
- Design implications for creating effective AI-powered chatbots for informed consent, including personalized assistance and power dynamics management.
- Demonstration of new opportunities for AI-enabled consent experiences beyond research settings.
