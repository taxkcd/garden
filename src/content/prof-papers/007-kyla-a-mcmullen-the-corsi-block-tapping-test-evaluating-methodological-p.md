# 007 · The Corsi Block-Tapping Test: Evaluating methodological practices with an eye towards modern digital frameworks

## At a glance

- **Professor:** Kyla A. McMullen
- **Institution:** University of Florida
- **Paper:** [The Corsi Block-Tapping Test: Evaluating methodological practices with an eye towards modern digital frameworks](https://doi.org/10.1016/j.chbr.2021.100099)
- **Authors:** Terek Arce, Kyla McMullen
- **Year:** 2021

## Paper overview

This paper systematically reviews 39 studies of the Corsi Block-Tapping Test (CBT), a widely used spatial memory test, to evaluate how consistently the test is administered and reported. It highlights the lack of methodological standardization, especially with the rise of digital CBT versions, and argues for the development of a standardized, open-source digital CBT framework that can improve reliability and comparability across studies.

### Why it matters

**Research problem:** The Corsi Block-Tapping Test has been widely used to measure spatial memory, but suffers from inconsistent methodologies, lack of standardization, and poor reporting of key parameters, especially with the emergence of digital versions. This inconsistency hampers reproducibility, comparability, and understanding of spatial memory.

**Why it matters:** Spatial memory is critical for everyday functioning and is studied across psychology, medicine, and human-computer interaction. Reliable and standardized CBT methods are essential for valid assessment, diagnosis, and research into spatial memory and related cognitive functions. Without standardization, results are difficult to compare or interpret, limiting scientific progress.

**Key contributions:**

- Comprehensive systematic review of modern CBT methodological practices over 20 years.
- Detailed analysis of key CBT parameters (block color, size, shape; board properties; spatial paths; administration).
- Identification of widespread variability and poor reporting in CBT methodologies.
- Discussion of equivalence issues between physical 3D and digital 2D CBT versions.
- Proposal for a standardized, open-source digital CBT framework supporting flexibility and reproducibility.
