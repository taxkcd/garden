# 006 · The Potential of Diverse Youth as Stakeholders in Identifying and Mitigating Algorithmic Bias for a Future of Fairer AI

## At a glance

- **Professor:** Amy E. Ogan

- **Authors:** Jaemarie Solyst, Ellia Yang, Shixian Xie, Amy Ogan, Jessica Hammer, Motahhare Eslami
- **Year:** 2023

## Paper overview

This study explores how diverse youth, including those from marginalized backgrounds, can recognize and address biases in AI systems. It challenges assumptions that youth lack the technical or ethical understanding to engage with AI fairness and shows that with support from peers and parents, youth can actively participate in identifying and mitigating algorithmic harms. The research involved workshops with youth and parents to understand their perceptions of AI bias and their ideas for designing fairer AI systems.

### Why it matters

**Research problem:** Youth are frequent users of AI-driven technologies but are underrepresented as stakeholders in identifying and mitigating algorithmic bias. There are prevailing notions that youth cannot understand the technical or ethical complexities of AI or should be protected from serious topics related to bias and injustice, which limits their participation in responsible AI efforts.

**Why it matters:** AI systems can perpetuate societal biases causing harm, especially to underrepresented groups including youth from marginalized communities. Since youth regularly interact with AI and are impacted by its biases, involving them as stakeholders is crucial for creating fairer AI systems and empowering them as agents of change.

**Key contributions:**

- Empirical evidence that youth can perceive and articulate complex notions of algorithmic bias and fairness.
- Demonstration that youth ideate diverse and nuanced designs for surfacing and mitigating AI harms.
- Identification of the role of parental and peer support in enhancing youth engagement with AI fairness.
- Challenge to common assumptions that youth lack technical or ethical capacity to participate in responsible AI.
- Proposal for youth-inclusive AI auditing systems that foster AI literacy and ethical awareness.
