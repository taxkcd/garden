# 027 · Generative AI Health Assistants in Modern Healthcare: Drivers and Barriers to Adoption

- **Authors:** Zainab Al-Lataifeh, Mark A. Harris, James Smith, Amita Goyal Chin
- **Year:** 2026

## Paper overview

This study explores why patients may or may not adopt generative AI health assistants (GAIHAs), which provide personalized medical support and information. It develops a new framework combining trust, perceived benefits, risks, and social factors to understand adoption intentions. The research finds that trust and perceived benefits encourage adoption, while privacy concerns and resistance to change hinder it. The study offers recommendations for developers and healthcare providers to improve patient acceptance of these AI tools.

### Why it matters

**Research problem:** Despite rapid technological advances and increasing availability of generative AI health assistants, patient adoption remains uncertain due to insufficient understanding of the drivers and barriers influencing their acceptance.

**Why it matters:** GAIHAs have the potential to transform healthcare delivery by improving patient engagement and personalized care, but low adoption limits their impact. Understanding adoption factors is critical to designing AI health tools that patients trust and use effectively.

**Key contributions:**

- Development of an integrated EVF-DOI-IB framework to comprehensively capture both drivers and barriers to GAIHA adoption.
- Extension of existing adoption models by incorporating innovation barriers like resistance to change and privacy concerns alongside trust and perceived benefits.
- Empirical evidence highlighting the critical role of interpersonal communication and relative advantage in building trust.
- Application of the valence framework to healthcare AI, balancing perceived risks and benefits in adoption decisions.
