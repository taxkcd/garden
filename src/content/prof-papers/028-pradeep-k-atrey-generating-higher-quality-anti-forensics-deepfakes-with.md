# 028 · Generating Higher-Quality Anti-Forensics DeepFakes with Adversarial Sharpening Mask

- **Year:** 2025

## Paper overview

This paper presents a novel method to create DeepFake images that can evade detection by forensic algorithms while maintaining high visual quality. The approach uses adversarial sharpening masks generated by two GAN-based networks to disguise DeepFakes as authentic, sharpened images, improving both undetectability and image realism.

### Why it matters

**Research problem:** DeepFake images can be detected by forensic algorithms, but attackers use anti-forensics techniques to evade detection, often sacrificing image quality. The challenge is to generate DeepFakes that are both hard to detect and visually convincing.

**Why it matters:** DeepFakes pose serious threats by spreading misinformation and manipulating public opinion. Improving anti-forensics techniques exposes vulnerabilities in forensic detectors, helping to develop stronger defenses and protect society from malicious DeepFake use.

**Key contributions:**

- Development of a novel two-module GAN framework (FDN and VEN) for DeepFake anti-forensics.
- Introduction of adversarial sharpening masks that disguise DeepFakes as sharpened authentic images.
- Use of MobileViT2 blocks in VEN to enhance visual quality and undetectability.
- Comprehensive evaluation on multiple benchmark datasets showing superior undetectability and image quality.
- Ablation studies demonstrating the importance of freezing parameters in FDN and the role of MobileViT2 blocks.
