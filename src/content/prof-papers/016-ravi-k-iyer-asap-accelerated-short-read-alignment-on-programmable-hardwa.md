# 016 · ASAP: Accelerated Short-Read Alignment on Programmable Hardware

- **Authors:** Subho S. Banerjee, Mohamed El-Hadedy, Jong Bin Lim, Zbigniew T. Kalbarczyk, Deming Chen, Steven S. Lumetta, Ravishankar K. Iyer
- **Year:** 2018

## Paper overview

This paper presents ASAP, a hardware accelerator implemented on FPGA to speed up the computation of Levenshtein distance (edit-distance) for short-read alignment in genomic data analysis. ASAP uses circuit propagation delays to approximate edit-distance calculations, achieving significant speedups and energy savings compared to CPU and previous hardware methods, while maintaining alignment accuracy.

### Why it matters

**Research problem:** Short-read alignment, a critical step in genomic data analysis, is computationally expensive because computing the Levenshtein distance (LD) between sequences dominates runtime (50-70%) in popular alignment tools. Existing methods are slow and energy-inefficient for the massive data generated by next-generation sequencing.

**Why it matters:** Efficient processing of large-scale genomic data is essential for applications like variant discovery, genome-wide association studies, and personalized medicine. Improving alignment speed and energy efficiency enables handling of growing sequencing data volumes and accelerates biological insights.

**Key contributions:**

- Proposed an approximation algorithm for LD computation that preserves total ordering of distances, enabling accelerated alignment without loss of accuracy.
- Mapped LD computation parameters (gap penalties) to circuit propagation delays, allowing tuning of accelerator performance to data characteristics.
- Implemented ASAP accelerator on FPGA using delay elements and clock gating to save energy by disabling unused computations.
- Integrated ASAP with the SNAP short-read aligner, achieving nearly 2× speedup in end-to-end alignment runtime.
- Demonstrated up to 200× speedup over CPU baseline and 50× over prior RaceLogic FPGA designs, with significant energy efficiency gains.
